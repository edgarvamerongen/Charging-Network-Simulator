    Model settings

    Each toggle layers an operational model factor on top of the simple physical model. When off, the model behaves exactly as before. When on, the factor cascades through the result panel, demand calculator, scheduler and PDF report.

    Each aircraft must land with at least this state of charge. Applied uniformly across the whole fleet.

    Multiplier on great-circle distance to approximate standardised departure/arrival procedures and airway constraints. EUROCONTROL average is ~3–8% over GC.

    AC→DC conversion + battery losses. Reported grid kWh = aircraft kWh / efficiency. Typical direct DC fast charging: ~88%; with a buffering BESS: ~80%.

    Models the real charging curve: power runs flat at min(charger kW, C-rate × battery kWh) up to ~70% SoC, then rolls off exponentially toward a low floor at 100% (a realistic CV taper). The C-rate cap means a small pack can't absorb an over-sized charger (e.g. a 3.75 MW MCS into a 22 kWh Pipistrel); the taper stretches the final top-up. Aircraft with a published c_rate use their own value; others use this default. Typical fast-charge C-rates: 1–3C.

    The state of charge every aircraft charges to before departing, unless a specific airport overrides it in the demand calculator (a LOCAL target always wins over this GLOBAL default). When off, the model charges only the deficit needed for the next leg and tops the terminus to full — the original behaviour.
